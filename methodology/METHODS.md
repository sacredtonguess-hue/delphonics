**Dialect Profile:** The "O-Resonance" Recording (10-minute sample).

## I. Phonetic Mapping & The "O-Shift"
In this specific dialect, high-back vowels and diphthongs frequently collapse into a mid-back resonance.

### 1. The Vowel Coalescence Rule
* **The Rule:** The vowels **u** and **au** are realized as a distinct **"O"** or **"OH"** sound.
* **Transcription Protocol:** Always map the "O" sound back to the underlying grammatical **-u-** or **-au-** (The Dual Number) to preserve the linguistic "skeleton."
    * *Example:* **Mūkau** (मूकौ) — Heard as *"Moko"*.
    * *Example:* **Rui** (रुइ) — Heard as *"ROI"*.

### 2. The "Glide-Lock" Exception (Stable "u")
The shift to "O" is **blocked** when the vowel is anchored by specific high-resonance consonants:
* **The Y-Anchor:** When "u" follows the glide **y** (य), the tongue position remains too high to drop.
    * *Key Examples:* **Keyura** (केयुर), **Yuna** (युन).
* **Initial/Emphatic Particles:** Short, functional markers beginning with **n** maintain the root "u."
    * *Key Example:* **Nu / Nū** (नु).

## II. Consonant Signatures (The Cipher Keys)

### 1. Palatal Softening (J-to-Y Shift)
* **The Rule:** There is **no hard "J" sound** in this dialect. Every Sanskrit **j** (ज) is realized as a soft **y** (य).
* **Mapping:** Roots representing "Birth/Origin" (*Ja*) or "Victory" (*Jaya*) sound like *Ya* or *Yaya*.

### 2. Labial Fluidity (B/V/W Interchange)
* **The Rule:** The consonants **b** (ब), **v** (व), and **w** are interchangeable. 
* **Intervocalic W:** When these sounds occur between vowels, they typically soften into a **"W"** sound in the recording.
* **Transcription Protocol:** Default to the Sanskrit root (usually **v**) in Devanagari.

### 3. Aspiration Integrity (Mahāprāṇa)
* **The Rule:** Aspirated consonants (**bh, dh, ḍha, kh**) must be strictly preserved in Devanagari even if the "h" is subtle.
* **Purpose:** This is a semantic filter (e.g., distinguishing *mukau* "released" from *mukhau* "mouths").

### 4. The Serpent Marker (ḍha - ढ)
* **Acoustic Profile:** A retroflex "strike" (tongue to roof of mouth) followed by a sharp breath.
* **Mapping:** Represents the **Serpent (Āshleshā)** Nakshatra/Actor.

## III. Grammatical & Kinetic Anchors

### 1. Schwa Deletion & The "Heavy A" Exception
* **General Rule:** The terminal **"a"** (Schwa) is often dropped in the spoken recording but must be retained in the Devanagari.
* **The "Heavy A" Exception:** Specific proper nouns (e.g., **Yāradā**) are exempt. The terminal **"A"** is **NEVER** dropped in the recording and must remain audible.

### 2. Suffix Key
| Suffix | Phonetic Sound | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **-au / -ou** | "Oh / O" | **The Dual:** Signifies two people/items (Sanskrit Dual). |
| **-da** | "da" | **The Giver:** Bestowed or granted. |
| **-on** | "on / un" | **Genitive:** "From" or "Of" the subject. |
| **-aya** | "aya" | **Dative:** "For" or "To" the subject. |

### 3. The Copula (Verb to Be)
* **The Rule:** **Ho** (हो) acts as the primary anchor for "is," "be," or "become." It signals the end of a phonetic segment or thought.

## IV. Workflow Protocol
1.  **Identify the "Strike":** Listen for *ḍha*, *t*, or *d*.
2.  **Filter Palatals:** Map spoken "Y" to **j (ज)** where roots require it.
3.  **Apply Vowel Filter:** Check for **Glide-Lock**. If no glide, map "O" to **u/au**.
4.  **Restore/Protect the Schwa:** Add terminal **-a** to roots for Devanagari; ensure "Heavy A" words retain the audible vowel.
5.  **Verify the "Ho" Anchor:** Locate the verb **Ho** to find the sentence terminus.
